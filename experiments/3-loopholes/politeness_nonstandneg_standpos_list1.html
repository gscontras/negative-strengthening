Welcome!

In this survey, you will read a series of short stories and your task is to evaluate what the person wanted to communicate. You will encounter people with two different interaction styles: one type of person who always sticks to their word and commits to what they say, and another type of person who often retracts what they said. Let's take a look at an example of the different interaction styles.

The person who always sticks to their word would say the following: "I finished some of our joint work"

You respond: "I was expecting you to finish all of it."

And they confirm : "I know, but I tried my best."

 

Alternatively, the person who often retracts what they said may say: "I finished some of our joint work."

You respond: "I was expecting you to finish all of it."

And they deny: "I never said that I didn't finish all of it."

 

For each story, we ask you to judge what the person wanted to communicate. Your answer can range from 1 to 7. In the following example, 1 represents "gorgeous" and 7 "ugly".

You ask a friend: How do I look? Your friend tells you:

"You are not gorgeous"

Please judge: According to your friend, your are...
gorgeous ugly
